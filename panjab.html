Explore the State: Punjab

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a Sikh gurdwara (place of worship) located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the holiest Gurdwara of Sikhism and one of the most important pilgrimage sites in the world. The temple is a significant symbol of Sikhism and is known for its architectural beauty and its message of peace and equality.

The Golden Temple was founded by the fourth Sikh Guru, Guru Ram Das, in the 16th century. The temple was built on a sacred tank called Amrit Sarovar, which means "Pool of Immortality." The temple is built in a four-tiered style, with the topmost tier being covered in gold. The temple is surrounded by a pool of water, which is considered sacred by Sikhs. The Golden Temple is a place of worship for Sikhs from all over the world. It is also a popular tourist destination, with millions of visitors each year. The temple is open to people of all faiths and is a symbol of peace and brotherhood.
